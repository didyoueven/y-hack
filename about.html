Welcome to Y-Hack 2013.

What is Y-Hack?

Y-Hack is a National Hackathon competition hosted by and at Yale University. The goal of the event is to bring together student hackers and entrepreneurs together with experienced industrial innovators.

What can I win?

We offer three development tracks: free development track with no API, the Microsoft Bing track, and the Intel HTML5 track.
For each of the two API development tracks, a $3500 sponsored prize will be given to the top chosen project, $1000 for the second and $500 for third.
For the free development track, there will be five placement awards, all sponsored by our generous donors. First place will receive $3500, $1500 for second, $750 for third, $500 for fourth, and $100 for fifth.

Can I come?

Absolutely! Any University student is available to enter Y-Hack with confirmed online registration. Please note that the specific API development tracks are limited in number.

I don't code, what about me?

You've found the perfect place and time to learn! Anyone can come, but just remember that we'll only reimburse travel if you actually demo a project.
